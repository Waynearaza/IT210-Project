Banaue Rice Terraces

Banaue, Ifugao

Stairway to the Sky

The Banaue Rice Terraces are a testament to the engineering marvel and cultural endurance of the Ifugao people.

Carved into the mountains of Ifugao by the ancestors of the indigenous people more than 2,000 years ago, these terraces were built with minimal equipment, largely by hand. They were designed to provide a sustainable irrigation system, catching water from the rainforests above the terraces.

A Living Cultural Landscape

Unlike many historic ruins, the Banaue Rice Terraces are not just a monument of the past; they are a living, breathing landscape. To this day, locals still plant rice and vegetables on the terraces, maintaining the ancient traditions of their forefathers.

When to Visit

The appearance of the terraces changes with the seasons. If you want to see them at their greenest, visit between April and May. For the golden harvest season, June and July are the best months.
