My Honest Review of Working Fully Remote

"Three years in, remote work has changed my relationship with productivity, creativity, and the concept of a 'work day' entirely. Not all of it was good — and that's worth talking about."

When I first transitioned to fully remote work, I thought I had hacked the system. No commute, endless coffee from my own kitchen, and the ability to write code in sweatpants. But as the months turned into years, the reality of untethered work began to set in.

The Blurring Lines

The biggest challenge I faced wasn't a lack of productivity—it was the inability to turn it off. When your office is five feet from your bed, the physical boundary between "work time" and "personal time" disappears. I found myself pushing commits at 11:00 PM simply because my laptop was nearby.

"The flexibility of remote work is a double-edged sword. You have the freedom to work whenever you want, which easily morphs into working all the time."

Finding the Rhythm

Overcoming the isolation and burnout required strict personal infrastructure. I started enforcing hard cut-offs, replacing my morning commute with a morning walk, and prioritizing asynchronous communication to minimize meeting fatigue. Remote work is incredible, but it demands a level of self-management and boundary-setting that no one teaches you in a traditional office.
